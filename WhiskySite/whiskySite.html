Cyrus' Reviews

Redbreast 12 Year

Colour: Quite Dark for a 12 year, I'd be curious if there's colouring added. The colour of apple juice from concentrate.

Nose: heavy heavy sherry influence. Smelled differently in the boston round, but in the glencairn I get dark fruits, raisins, figs, prunes etc. There are also notes of chocolate, and some lighter fruit notes, such as older red delicious apples, when they become crumbly.

Palate: WOW, That is intense sherry. I get a slight difference from scotch, but this is quite unlike the cheaper Irish whiskies I've tried. There is a slight ethanol bite initially, but throughout that sip you get raisins and fruit from the start all the way to the end. This whisky definitely warms you up, and drinks well for its ABV. Quite full mouthfeel, but the malty notes do pair very well with the sherry notes.

Finish: The heavier mouthfeel does carry over, and the whisky itself finishes with a decent ethanol bite. Those sherry notes stay, with the raisin notes really hitting at the back of the tongue, absolutely caressing and serenading my olfactory system. Once those sherry notes, it ends with a very familiar malty note, as I get when I drink scotch.

Final: Wow does this whisky drink above its price range, super full mouth feel, delicious and complex sherry notes leave, and holds its weight at ABV making hold its weight without being a pushover. Overall incredibly impressed, and this tempts me to get into the world of irish whisky. This follows what I expected to be the archetypal Irish whisky, but with a ton more sherry.

Score: 6.5/10
